About

Built by someone who's been on both sides of the contract.

Twenty years in B2B marketing. Four businesses built. One very clear view of how content agencies should operate.

The Journey

In the Feed is my fourth business.

I started as a Technical Virtual Assistant. Before drag and drop existed, I was building CRMs, WordPress sites, landing pages, and sales funnels by hand. For coaches, authors, and speakers. Ten years of that.

I learned how to build the marketing system before I understood the philosophy behind it. That turned out to be the most useful education I never paid for.

After ten years, I learned the philosophy. Opened a digital marketing agency. Ran it for a year before realising that doing everything was doing nothing. So I put the energy where it mattered: lead generation for tech and SaaS companies.

In 2024 I made a deliberate choice. I put my own business down to go full time inside an established content agency. The plan made sense. Learn the model from inside. Help scale something already moving.

What I walked into was not what I signed up for. Clients were being misled. Work was being misrepresented. The results on paper didn't match the reality on the ground. I stayed a year trying to make it work. But there is a difference between persistence and complicity.

I left. Then I built this.

I built In the Feed because I watched too many founders get sold content and delivered noise. The gap wasn't the writing. It was everything around it. No system for reviewing metrics. No tool for engagement. No shared approval process.

Agencies made big promises to close the deal, then underdelivered the moment the contract was signed. I knew I could do better. Not for everyone. For founders willing to invest three months and let the engine run properly.

Why I Built It

Content agencies were selling content only.

No metrics layer. No engagement system. No accountability beyond the invoice. They optimised for their own output, not your pipeline. That was the gap.

In the Feed was built to close it. A real system: strategy, production, distribution, reporting, and a founder who stays in the room. Not a handoff. A partnership.

I keep the agency small by design. It means I'm in the weeds with you. Available. Communicating. Making sure your business is seen and heard across LinkedIn, your blog, and video, without you having to manage any of it.

I work and live as a nomad, running In the Feed from wherever I happen to be. The systems I build for clients are the same ones I rely on myself. That's not a philosophy. That's just how it works.

Why FemTech

I'm not just working in this space. I've lived it.

I'm a GenX woman. I'm post-menopausal. I started perimenopause at 35. Everyone told me I was too young. I wasn't. I just wasn't the norm.

I was 45 when I found out there are over 100 symptoms of menopause. And almost no standardised relief for most of them. Half the world is women. The gap between that fact and the state of care is still staggering.

That's not background. That's why I care about what FemTech founders are building. And it's why I know how to talk about it in a way that doesn't sound like a press release or a wellness brand.

Your enterprise buyers need to trust you. I understand what's at stake on both sides of that conversation.
